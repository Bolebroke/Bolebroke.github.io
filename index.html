What is drag hunting?

Drag hunting is the sport of using hounds to follow an artificial scent that is dragged along the ground by a trail layer. In the Bolebroke beagle pack we use an aniseed scent. When drag hunting, the trail layer gets a head start and will sometimes jog as far as 15km through farmland, woodland, moors, marshes and anywhere else we've been invited. Only the trail layer knows where they've been, so we have to use the hounds noses to follow the trail and find the trail layer! The followers can be as active as they choose - keep up with the hounds, or find a nice vantage point to watch them from a distance. We're a social bunch, and our followers often enjoy long conversations and making friends whilst watching the hounds work.

We only ever follow a plant-based aniseed scent, and we are always focused on the welfare of both our hounds and the wildlife that we interact with

Contact us:

If you want to get involved or find out more information, send a message to info@bolebroke.co.uk

Our meets usually last about 3 hours and start with drinks and a sausage roll, and finish with tea and cake. We cover a large part of Kent so you might find a meet near you and it is a wonderful way to see parts of the countryside you wouldn't normally have access to.

We welcome all visitors, be they newcomers, experts or anywhere inbetween. If you want to meet our hounds, we would love to meet you. Polite dogs on leads are welcome by invitation but please meantion this beforehand.
